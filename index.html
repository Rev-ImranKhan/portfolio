ABOUT ME

Built without a safety net.

I'm a self-taught engineer who chose a different path. No internships. No mentors. No one telling me what to build. Just a laptop, an internet connection, and a relentless drive to solve real problems using AI — completely from the ground up. I thrive in autonomy, but I know the best products are built through deep collaboration with designers, PMs, and fellow engineers.

For the last two years, I've been fully self-directed — designing, building, and shipping production-ready GenAI applications. I didn't wait for a job to teach me; I taught myself by shipping 30+ live projects across agriculture, education, legal access, and hiring.

I don't build to fill a resume — I build because I see gaps that need fixing. Farmers need crop advice (FasalGuru). Students need quality tutoring (ShikshaBot). Citizens need legal aid and scheme discovery (Nyay AI, Sarkari Yojana). Recruiters need better screening (AI Resume Screener). I identified these problems and built working solutions — end to end.

I also founded HireVix — a remote-work hiring platform. While the business didn't scale, it gave me something no course ever could: real-world founder experience. I learned the brutal gap between "code that works" and "a product people actually pay for." That experience fundamentally changed how I build — I now think in terms of time-to-market, user impact, and business viability, not just tech features.

🧠 Founder Mindset

I've had an entrepreneur's mindset since childhood — I don't wait for instructions; I find the gaps, own the outcome, and get it done. A task isn't complete until it's actually solving the problem it was meant to solve. I don't quit until it's finished. That resilience brought me here — 30+ projects, no safety net, and a track record of shipping against every odd. I'm looking for a role where I can bring that same founder-level grit, ownership, and execution speed to solve hard AI problems for a company I genuinely believe in.

🚀 A portfolio of product experiments, not just projects. Here's the truth — every single one of these 11 projects was built with the same founder mindset that drove me to start HireVix. I didn't build them to impress recruiters or fill a GitHub grid. I built them because I saw a gap, formulated a hypothesis, scoped an MVP, launched it, and observed how real users interacted with it.

Some of them are raw. Some need polish. But every single one is a live product experiment — designed for real people, deployed end-to-end, and built with the belief that "if this gains traction, it could become a business." This isn't a project showcase — this is my entrepreneurial journey in 11 acts.

🧠 My Philosophy

"I don't just use AI — I challenge it. When the model hits a wall, I find the workaround. When it hesitates, I push it further. Solving those stuck moments is what drives me. This isn't just a job; it's my element. If I'm not building, tinkering, or interacting with AI on my laptop, I feel disconnected. I build because I can't imagine not building."

🚀 What's Next

I have many more ideas waiting to be built — this is just the beginning. My pipeline is full of concepts that I believe can create real impact. I plan to keep building, keep shipping, and keep executing.

→ Problem-first, not tech-first. I start with who has this problem and why it hurts, not which stack looks impressive.
